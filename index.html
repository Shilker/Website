The Seahorse


The dad carries around 100-1000 eggs and delivers the babies (the only species on earth) about three weeks later.



The slowest moving fish in the world is the dwarf seahorse which can only cover 5 feet in one hour.

Seahorses can camouflage themselves, making it hard to spot them.


Seahorses' eyes move independently from each other making it easier to spot predators.

Seahorses are one of the only types of fish that can swim upright but they can easily die from exhaustion.
